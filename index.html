GeoScale 2020

Workshop for GIScience 2020

1st International Workshop for Scalable Geographic Methods and Computing Platforms



Pozna≈Ñ, Poland

September 15-18, 2020



The 1st International Workshop for Scalable Geographic Methods and Computing Platforms (GeoScale 2020) is particularly timely given the prominence of GIS technology and data in every walk of life today. The workshop particularly focuses on research and science behind developing scalable solutions for GIS. We expect the innovation and work should demonstrate research that requires large-systems with the capability to deploy in real-world scenarios. Also, theoretical work addressing such challenges would be a great addition to the workshop scope.

The workshop scope is novel for an academic conference, with a focus on the rigor of scalable aspects of computing, distributed geospatial systems, location-based edge computing, multi-source data fusion, geospatial intelligence platforms, high-performance computing for GIS, sensing in the wild, and other related disciplines.

We will be organizing a panel alongside paper presentations / keynote. We seek to invite eminent people from industry, academia, and laboratories.



Topics


Suggested topics of interest, but not limited to are as following:

  • Scalable geospatial computing platform
  • Computing architecture for GIS
  • Hybrid and distributed computing for large scale space-time compute
  • Multivariate data processing in real-time
  • FPGA and edge computing for GIS
  • Distributed GIS algorithms, methods, and data structure
  • Theoretical analysis and methods
  • System design of GIS platform with focus on large-scale computation
  • Large-scale data collection frameworks
  • Distributed ML for spatio-temporal data on large scale



Submissions


All papers must be original and not simultaneously submitted to another journal or conference. The following paper categories are welcome:

  • Full papers (up to 10 pages)
  • Short papers and Position Papers (up to 4 pages)
  • Demo Papers (up to 4 pages)
Papers must be in GIScience format (US Letter size, 8.5 x 11 inches) including text, figures and references. Accepted papers will be published in the digital library under the condition that at least one author has registered for both the main conference and the workshop, attends the workshop, and presents the accepted paper in the workshop.
